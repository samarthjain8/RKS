Understanding Rights Issues

What Is a Rights Issue?

A rights issue is a mechanism by which a listed company raises additional capital by offering new shares to its existing shareholders — in proportion to their current holdings — at a price that is typically at a discount to the prevailing market price. Shareholders have the right, but not the obligation, to subscribe to these new shares.

The term "rights" refers precisely to this preferential entitlement — existing shareholders are given the first right to participate in any new equity issuance before shares are offered to outside investors. This protects them from dilution if they choose to exercise, and gives them optionality if they choose to renounce.

"A rights issue is the most shareholder-democratic way for a listed company to raise capital — offering existing investors the choice to maintain their stake or monetise their rights."

Compared to other equity raising mechanisms available to listed companies — QIPs, preferential allotments, or FPOs — rights issues are unique in that they offer shares exclusively to current shareholders first, making them the most equitable capital raising tool and one that regulators and institutional investors view most favourably from a governance standpoint.

How a Rights Issue Works

1
Board & Shareholder Approval

Board approves the rights issue and convenes an EGM or passes a resolution by postal ballot for shareholder approval under Companies Act and SEBI ICDR regulations.

2
Record Date & Entitlement

A record date is fixed. Shareholders holding shares on this date are entitled to receive rights in proportion to their existing holdings — e.g., 1 new share for every 3 shares held.

3
Letter of Offer (LOO) Filing

The Letter of Offer — a detailed disclosure document similar to a prospectus — is filed with SEBI and the stock exchanges. SEBI reviews and issues observations.

4
Rights Entitlement Credits (RECs)

Rights Entitlements are credited to eligible shareholders' demat accounts as tradeable instruments — allowing shareholders to subscribe, renounce partially, or sell their REs on the exchange.

5
Subscription Window

The issue opens for a minimum of 15 days. Shareholders and renounced RE buyers apply through ASBA/RRNB mechanism during this window.

6
Allotment & Listing

Post-closure, allotment is made to subscribers. Unsubscribed shares may be offered to promoters (if authorised). New shares listed on exchanges within prescribed timelines.

Issue Formats

Types of Rights Issue

Standard

Fully Subscribed Rights Issue

A standard rights issue where the company aims to raise the full issue amount from existing shareholders — with a standby underwriting arrangement to cover any unsubscribed portions, ensuring the company receives its targeted capital even if some shareholders do not participate.

  • Full issue amount targeted from shareholders
  • Underwriting / standby arrangement recommended
  • Unsubscribed shares offered to promoter (if approved)
  • Most common structure for listed companies
Fast-Track

Fast-Track Rights Issue (FTRI)

An expedited rights issue available to listed companies meeting specific track record criteria — allowing SEBI review and issue completion in a compressed timeline, bypassing the standard observation letter process. Introduced by SEBI in 2010 and enhanced further in 2020 for greater efficiency.

  • Available to companies listed for 3+ years
  • No SEBI observation letter required
  • Total timeline reduced to ~25 days from filing
  • Must file draft LOO with SEBI and exchanges simultaneously
Composite

Composite Rights Issue

A rights issue that includes both equity shares and other securities — such as warrants, preference shares, or NCDs — offered as a composite package. Allows companies to raise equity and debt simultaneously, or to offer upside optionality through attached warrants while issuing shares at current prices.

  • Equity + warrant package structure common
  • NCD + equity composite for debt-laden companies
  • Each component priced and disclosed separately
  • Requires separate regulatory approvals for each instrument
Regulatory Qualification

Eligibility & Conditions
for a Rights Issue

Under SEBI (Issue of Capital and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2018, a listed company must satisfy certain conditions before it can make a rights issue. These conditions are designed to ensure that only companies with adequate compliance track records and governance standards are permitted to raise capital from public shareholders.

For companies that do not satisfy all standard eligibility conditions, SEBI provides limited relief provisions — though these come with additional disclosure and regulatory scrutiny. RKS Investments assesses eligibility as the first step of every engagement and advises on the most appropriate issue structure accordingly.

Notably, a rights issue does not require the company to be currently profitable — making it a viable capital raising option for companies in growth or recovery phases that may not qualify for an FPO or QIP.

Execution Framework

The Rights Issue Process

A standard rights issue takes 3–5 months from mandate to listing. Fast-Track Rights Issues can be completed in under 30 days from SEBI filing. Here is how RKS Investments manages every stage.

01

Mandate & Issue Structuring

We engage with the company's management and board to understand the capital need, intended use of proceeds, and timeline preferences. Together we determine the issue size, issue price (and discount to CMP), entitlement ratio, and whether a standard or fast-track process is appropriate. Eligibility is confirmed at this stage.

Issue sizing Pricing strategy Eligibility check Engagement letter
Duration: 1 – 2 Weeks
02

Team Assembly & Due Diligence

We coordinate the appointment of the Rights Issue Lead Manager (SEBI-registered Merchant Banker), Registrar to the Issue, Legal Counsel, and Banker to the Issue. A thorough due diligence of the company's financials, disclosures, and regulatory status is conducted to form the basis of the Letter of Offer.

Lead Manager appointment Registrar selection Legal due diligence Financial verification
Duration: 3 – 5 Weeks
03

Draft Letter of Offer (DLOF) Preparation

The Draft Letter of Offer — the primary disclosure document for the rights issue — is prepared. The LOO covers company background, risk factors, use of proceeds, entitlement ratio, issue price determination, financial statements, and all required SEBI disclosures. It is drafted by the Lead Manager and reviewed exhaustively before filing.

Business overview Risk factors Use of proceeds Pricing justification
Duration: 4 – 6 Weeks
04

SEBI & Exchange Filing

The DLOF is filed simultaneously with SEBI and the stock exchanges. SEBI reviews the document and issues observations (queries) that must be addressed before the issue can open. For Fast-Track Rights Issues, SEBI observations are not required — filing with the exchanges is sufficient before opening the issue.

SEBI DLOF submission Exchange filing SEBI observation response FTRI: no observation required
Duration: 4 – 8 Weeks (Standard); 0 Weeks (FTRI)
05

Record Date & Rights Entitlements (REs)

A record date is fixed and announced to the exchanges. On and after the record date, Rights Entitlements (REs) are credited to eligible shareholders' demat accounts as separate tradeable securities. Shareholders can choose to subscribe, renounce (sell on the exchange), or apply for additional shares (if permitted by the scheme).

Record date announcement RE credit to demat RE trading window ASBA / RRNB mechanism
Duration: 1 – 2 Weeks
06

Issue Open — Subscription Window

The issue opens for a minimum of 15 days and a maximum of 30 days. Shareholders apply through the ASBA or RRNB mechanism — their funds are blocked in bank accounts rather than transferred upfront, reducing capital risk. We manage investor communications, ASBA coordination, and subscription tracking throughout the window.

Minimum 15 days open ASBA / RRNB applications Investor communication Subscription monitoring
Duration: 15 – 30 Days
07

Allotment, Refund & Listing

Post-closure, the Registrar finalises allotment. Subscribed shares are allotted within prescribed timelines; unsubscribed shares may be offered to promoters if shareholder approval was obtained. Allotted shares are credited to subscribers' demat accounts and listed on the exchanges — completing the rights issue cycle.

Allotment finalisation Unsubscribed shares to promoter Demat credit Exchange listing
Duration: 2 – 3 Weeks post-close
Pricing Mechanics

Issue Pricing &
Entitlement Ratio

The two critical design decisions in a rights issue are the issue price and the entitlement ratio. These parameters are deeply interconnected — and their combined effect determines the theoretical ex-rights price (TERP), the implied value of each rights entitlement, and how attractive the offer is to shareholders.

SEBI specifies a floor price for rights issues — based on the higher of the 26-week or 2-week volume-weighted average price (VWAP) of the shares on the exchange. Companies typically set the issue price at a 20–40% discount to the prevailing market price, striking a balance between capital raising certainty and limiting dilution to existing shareholders who subscribe.

The entitlement ratio determines how many new shares are offered per share currently held. A 1:2 ratio means one new share for every two existing shares — equivalent to a 33.3% dilution for non-subscribing holders. The ratio is calibrated to raise the required capital at the chosen issue price.

Compliance Landscape

Regulatory Requirements
for a Rights Issue

A rights issue involves compliance with multiple overlapping regulatory frameworks — securities law, company law, SEBI regulations, exchange listing requirements, and the Income Tax Act. Navigating these requirements simultaneously, within compressed timelines, demands deep regulatory expertise.

SEBI's 2020 circular significantly liberalised the rights issue process for listed companies — introducing tradeable Rights Entitlements (REs), the ASBA mechanism, and enhanced FTRI provisions that have dramatically reduced the time and cost of rights capital raising.

RKS Investments tracks regulatory changes in real time and ensures that every rights issue engagement is structured in full compliance with the latest SEBI, Companies Act, and exchange requirements — with no surprises in the regulatory approval process.

SEBI ICDR
Chapter IV — Rights Issue; SEBI (ICDR) Regulations, 2018 Primary regulation governing eligibility, pricing, disclosure, and process for listed company rights issues.
Companies Act
Section 62 — Further Issue of Share Capital Shareholder approval via EGM or postal ballot required. Board authorisation and special resolution for pricing below par or specific use of proceeds.
SEBI LODR
Continuous disclosure obligations — prior and post-issue Board meeting disclosure, record date announcement, DLOF filing, subscription status, allotment, and listing disclosures all required.
Exchange Listing
BSE and NSE listing agreement and listing application New shares require fresh listing application. Rights Entitlements also listed and traded for the RE trading window period.
Income Tax Act
Section 56(2)(x) — taxation of shares received at discount For NRI shareholders and certain resident investors, spread between FMV and issue price may be taxed under Section 56 as income from other sources.
FEMA
NRI and FPI shareholder participation — RBI/FEMA compliance Rights entitlements to NRI shareholders require compliance with FEMA 20R. FPIs can participate subject to sector-level FDI cap headroom.
Investor Decision Guide

Options Available to
Shareholders in a Rights Issue

Option A

Subscribe in Full

The shareholder exercises all their rights entitlements — subscribing to the full number of new shares they are entitled to at the issue price. Their proportionate ownership stake is maintained post-issue, with no dilution.

Outcome No dilution. Cash outflow equal to entitlement × issue price. Beneficial if company performs well post-raise.
Option B

Subscribe in Part

The shareholder exercises only a portion of their rights entitlements and lets the remainder lapse or sells them. Results in partial dilution proportional to the unexercised rights — but reduces capital outflow.

Outcome Partial dilution on unexercised REs. Flexibility to participate with limited capital. Unexercised REs lapse at close.
Option C

Renounce (Sell REs)

The shareholder sells their Rights Entitlements (REs) on the exchange during the RE trading window — receiving cash equal to the theoretical value of the rights, which partially compensates for the post-issue dilution they will experience.

Outcome Cash received from RE sale compensates for dilution. Net economic position approximately maintained if RE is priced at TERP.
Option D

Apply for Additional Shares

If the issue terms permit, shareholders can apply for shares in excess of their entitlement — seeking to acquire unsubscribed portions at the issue price. This is subject to availability and allotment on a pro-rata or lottery basis.

Outcome Opportunity to increase stake at discounted issue price. Additional allotment not guaranteed — depends on overall subscription level.
At a Glance

Rights Issue — Key Facts

Subscription Period

Minimum 15 Days, Maximum 30 Days

SEBI mandates the issue remain open for at least 15 working days, giving shareholders adequate time to make an informed decision. The company sets the closing date within the 30-day outer limit.

Minimum Subscription

90% of Issue Size Required

If less than 90% of the issue amount is subscribed by closing, the issue is deemed failed and all application monies must be refunded to applicants with interest at 15% per annum.

RE Trading Window

Rights Entitlements Are Tradeable

Post-SEBI 2020 circular, REs are credited to demat accounts and can be traded on the exchange during a specified window — allowing shareholders who do not wish to subscribe to monetise their rights.

Pricing Floor

SEBI Floor Price Formula

Issue price cannot be lower than the higher of: (a) 26-week VWAP on the stock exchange, or (b) 2-week VWAP on the stock exchange — as of the record date. Typically companies price at 20–40% below CMP.

Application Method

ASBA / RRNB Mechanism

Applications are made through the ASBA (Application Supported by Blocked Amount) system — funds remain blocked in the shareholder's bank account until allotment, reducing risk and processing friction.

Promoter Participation

Promoters Can Subscribe & Top Up

Promoters can subscribe to their full entitlement and, with shareholder approval, additionally subscribe to unsubscribed portions — allowing promoter holding to increase as a result of a rights issue without requiring open offer compliance (within permitted limits).

Fast-Track Timeline

FTRI Can Close in ~25 Days

For eligible companies, Fast-Track Rights Issues (FTRI) can be completed from filing to listing in approximately 25 working days — significantly faster than an FPO or QIP alternative for similar amounts.

NRI / FPI Participation

Foreign Shareholders Can Participate

NRI and FPI shareholders can receive and exercise rights entitlements, subject to FEMA compliance and sector-level FDI caps. REs can be renounced by non-participating foreign shareholders on the exchange.

Post-Issue Compliance

Listing Within 7 Working Days

Post closure of the subscription period, allotted shares must be listed on the stock exchanges within 7 working days — one of the fastest post-close timelines among listed company capital market transactions.
