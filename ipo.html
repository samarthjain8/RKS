Understanding IPOs

What Is an IPO?

An Initial Public Offering (IPO) is the process by which a privately held company offers its shares to the public for the first time, transitioning into a publicly listed entity on a recognized stock exchange.

Going public is one of the most significant milestones in a company's lifecycle. It provides access to large pools of capital, enhances brand visibility, creates liquidity for existing shareholders, and establishes a publicly traded currency for future acquisitions and employee compensation.

However, the IPO process is complex, highly regulated, and time-intensive. It demands careful preparation across legal, financial, regulatory, and investor relations dimensions — making the choice of an experienced advisor critical to success.

At RKS Investments, we bring deep transaction expertise and an extensive ecosystem of bankers, legal counsel, registrars, and investors to ensure your IPO is structured correctly and executed effectively.

◆

Capital for Growth

An IPO raises fresh equity capital that can fund expansion, acquisitions, debt repayment, and working capital needs without diluting control beyond necessity.

◆

Enhanced Credibility

Listed companies enjoy greater trust from customers, suppliers, lenders, and talent — a publicly traded status signals transparency and governance maturity.

◆

Shareholder Liquidity

Existing promoters, angel investors, and PE/VC funds can partially or fully exit through the Offer for Sale (OFS) component of the IPO.

◆

Ongoing Market Currency

A listed share becomes a powerful tool for employee ESOPs, future acquisitions via share swaps, and institutional investor partnerships.

◆

Regulatory Oversight & Governance

SEBI's listing obligations require ongoing disclosures, board governance, and audit standards — building long-term institutional investor confidence.

IPO Formats

Types of IPO We Handle

Mainboard

Mainboard IPO

For larger, established companies seeking listing on the main boards of BSE or NSE. Governed by SEBI (ICDR) Regulations, requiring a SEBI-registered Merchant Banker as Book Running Lead Manager.

  • Minimum post-issue paid-up capital of ₹10 Cr (BSE) / ₹25 Cr (NSE)
  • Book Building or Fixed Price mechanism
  • Red Herring Prospectus (RHP) filing with SEBI
  • Minimum 3-year track record required
  • Suitable for mid-to-large companies
SME

SME IPO

Designed for small and medium enterprises, listed on BSE SME or NSE Emerge platforms with relaxed eligibility norms, enabling smaller companies to access public capital markets.

  • Post-issue paid-up capital ₹1 Cr – ₹25 Cr
  • Minimum 2-year profitability track record (relaxed for certain sectors)
  • Market maker mandatory for BSE SME
  • Faster and more cost-effective process
  • Migration to mainboard possible after 2 years
OFS / Fresh Issue

Offer Structures

IPOs can be structured as Fresh Issues (raising new capital for the company), Offer for Sale (existing shareholder exit), or a combination of both — each with distinct regulatory and financial implications.

  • Fresh Issue: Proceeds go to the company
  • OFS: Proceeds go to selling shareholders
  • Combined: Optimises both objectives
  • Pre-IPO placement possible (up to 20% of issue size)
  • Reserved categories: QIB, NII, Retail
End-to-End Execution

The IPO Process

A typical IPO takes 9–18 months from initiation to listing. Here's how RKS Investments guides you through each stage.

01

IPO Readiness Assessment

We conduct a comprehensive evaluation of the company's financial health, governance structures, regulatory compliance posture, and capital requirements to determine whether an IPO is the right strategic move — and identify gaps to be addressed before filing.

Financial Review Promoter Background Sector Benchmarking Gap Analysis
Duration: 4 – 8 Weeks
02

Team Assembly & Mandate Structuring

We help assemble the full IPO team — Lead Manager (BRLM), registrar, legal counsel, statutory auditors, and PR advisors. The issue structure (size, Fresh Issue vs OFS, pricing band range) is finalised with all stakeholders.

BRLM Appointment Registrar Selection Legal Counsel Issue Structuring
Duration: 2 – 4 Weeks
03

Due Diligence & Document Preparation

Comprehensive legal, financial, and business due diligence is conducted. The Draft Red Herring Prospectus (DRHP) is prepared — a detailed document covering business overview, risk factors, financial statements, and use of proceeds — and submitted to SEBI for review.

Legal Due Diligence Financial Statements DRHP Drafting SEBI Filing
Duration: 10 – 16 Weeks
04

SEBI Observations & Pre-IPO Placement

SEBI reviews the DRHP and issues observations (queries) that must be addressed. Simultaneously, a pre-IPO placement of up to 20% of the issue may be conducted with anchor or institutional investors to build confidence before the public offering.

SEBI Query Responses Pre-IPO Placement Anchor Book Building
Duration: 4 – 10 Weeks
05

Roadshow & Investor Marketing

The management team conducts investor roadshows — meetings and presentations with institutional investors, HNIs, and retail brokers to generate interest and build the order book. We coordinate all marketing materials, analyst briefings, and media communications.

Investor Presentations Analyst Briefings Media PR Order Book Building
Duration: 2 – 3 Weeks
06

Subscription, Allotment & Listing

The IPO is open for public subscription for 3 working days. Post-closure, allotment is finalised, refunds processed, and shares credited to applicants' demat accounts. The company lists on the exchange — marking the beginning of its public market journey.

3-Day Subscription Window Allotment Finalisation Refund Processing Exchange Listing
Duration: 2 – 3 Weeks post-close
07

Post-Listing Compliance & Support

After listing, companies must comply with SEBI's Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements (LODR). We assist with quarterly result disclosures, corporate actions, investor grievance management, and ongoing regulatory filings for a smooth transition to listed-company operations.

LODR Compliance Quarterly Disclosures Corporate Actions Investor Relations
