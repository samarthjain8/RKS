Understanding Debt Advisory

What Is Debt Syndication?

Debt syndication is the process of arranging debt financing for a business by identifying, approaching, structuring, and negotiating with multiple lenders — banks, NBFCs, bond market investors, and alternative credit providers — to secure the most appropriate credit facility at optimal terms.

Unlike a bilateral bank relationship where a single lender provides the full facility, syndication spreads the loan across multiple lenders — reducing concentration risk, increasing the quantum of capital available, and introducing market-based competition that improves pricing and terms for the borrower.

"The cost of debt is the most visible number in a financing. But tenor, security, covenant flexibility, and drawdown structure often matter far more to a growing business."

A skilled debt advisor does far more than find a lender. We help structure the facility to align with your cash flows, negotiate covenants to preserve operational flexibility, optimise the security package to protect promoter assets, and ensure the financing supports — rather than constrains — your business strategy.

Capital Instruments

Debt Instruments
We Arrange

Different business needs call for different debt instruments. RKS Investments advises on and arranges the full spectrum of credit facilities — from vanilla term loans to structured bonds and mezzanine capital.

TL
Bank Debt

Term Loans

The most common debt instrument — a lump-sum credit disbursed upfront and repaid over a fixed schedule. Suitable for capital expenditure, project finance, and business acquisition funding.

  • Tenor: 3–15 years
  • Rate: Linked to MCLR / Repo rate
  • Security: Primary asset + collateral charge
  • Sources: PSU banks, private banks, NBFCs
  • Use: Capex, acquisition, refinancing
WC
Bank Debt

Working Capital Facilities

Revolving credit lines that fund day-to-day operations — inventory, receivables, and trade payables — structured as cash credit, overdraft, or bill discounting facilities.

  • CC / OD: Revolving, secured against current assets
  • Bill Discounting: Receivables-backed short-term
  • WCDL: Working Capital Demand Loan
  • LC / BG: Trade finance instruments
  • Renewal: Annual review basis
NCD
Capital Market

Non-Convertible Debentures

Debt securities issued to institutional investors through private placement or public issue. NCDs offer flexible tenor and coupon structures — and can be listed on BSE/NSE for liquidity.

  • Tenor: 1–10 years (structured as needed)
  • Coupon: Fixed or floating rate
  • Investors: Mutual funds, insurance, HNIs
  • Security: Secured (first/second charge) or unsecured
  • Listing: BSE/NSE debt segment optional
ECB
Cross-Border

External Commercial Borrowings

Foreign currency loans borrowed from overseas lenders — offering lower interest rates versus domestic debt, subject to RBI ECB framework guidelines on end-use, maturity, and all-in-cost ceilings.

  • Tenor: Minimum 3 years (track I); 10 years (track II)
  • Currency: USD, EUR, JPY, GBP
  • Hedging: Currency risk management mandatory
  • End-use: Capex, refinancing, working capital (track III)
  • Regulatory: RBI ECB framework compliance
FCCB
Hybrid

Foreign Currency Convertible Bonds

Hybrid instruments that start as foreign currency bonds but carry an option to convert into equity at a pre-determined price. Popular with listed companies seeking overseas capital with potential equity upside.

  • Listed on overseas exchange (Singapore, Luxembourg)
  • Coupon: Lower than straight debt — equity upside offset
  • Conversion: At premium to prevailing market price
  • Suitable for: Listed cos with growth equity story
  • Regulatory: RBI/SEBI/FEMA compliance
Mezz
Structured

Mezzanine & Structured Credit

Subordinated debt instruments that sit between senior debt and equity in the capital structure — offering higher yields to lenders and more flexibility to borrowers who have maxed senior credit capacity.

  • Form: OCD, OCPS, structured NCDs, sub-debt
  • Returns: Structured as IRR including PIK / equity kicker
  • Sources: AIFs, credit funds, NBFCs, family offices
  • Use: Bridge, LBO, promoter buyout, acquisition
  • Tenor: 2–5 years with bullet / PIK repayment

Public Sector Banks

SBI, Bank of Baroda, Punjab National Bank, and other PSU banks offer the largest credit limits at competitive rates — but require strong credit profiles, extensive documentation, and longer processing timelines.

  • Flexible collateral and covenant structures
  • Trade finance and forex services bundled

Non-Banking Financial Companies

NBFCs like Bajaj Finance, Piramal Capital, Edelweiss, and Aditya Birla Finance offer the most flexible structuring options — particularly for businesses with complex cash flows, promoter-led credits, or sector-specific needs.

  • Rate driven by credit rating

  • SEBI LODR compliance required for listed NCDs
Execution Framework

The Debt Syndication Process

A disciplined, lender-competitive debt process consistently delivers better pricing, covenants, and terms than a bilateral bank approach. Here is how RKS Investments runs every mandate.

01
Mandate & Credit Assessment
1 – 2 Weeks
Understand the business, financing need, purpose, repayment capacity, and existing credit facilities. Conduct preliminary credit assessment.
02
Financial Packaging
2 – 3 Weeks

03

Management meetings with lenders, site visits if required, and detailed financial Q&A. We prepare management and handle technical credit queries.

2 – 4 Weeks

06
Documentation & Legal
2 – 4 Weeks
Coordinate loan agreement, hypothecation deed, mortgage registration, board resolutions, and condition precedent fulfillment with legal counsel.
07
Disbursement & Compliance
Ongoing
First drawdown, end-use certification, covenant tracking, and ongoing lender communication. Annual review support and refinancing advisory as needed.

Lender Evaluation

What Lenders
Evaluate

Every lending decision comes down to a lender's confidence in two things: the borrower's ability to repay and the quality of security backing the facility. Understanding how lenders think is essential to structuring a successful debt raise.

RKS Investments prepares clients across every dimension lenders evaluate — from financial modelling and DSCR optimisation to promoter background checks and security structuring — so that your credit proposal is watertight before it reaches a lender's credit committee.

We also advise on which parameters to prioritise, negotiate, or compensate for — helping businesses secure debt even when certain metrics fall short of idealised thresholds.

DSCR
Min. 1.25x–1.50x (project finance); 1.10x+ (WC) Debt Service Coverage Ratio — primary repayment test
Leverage
Debt / EBITDA typically below 4x–5x for term debt Higher leverage acceptable with strong asset coverage
Promoter Track Record
Clean CIBIL, no wilful default, no pending litigation Critical — promoter credibility often overrides financials
Security Cover
1.25x–1.5x security cover on primary + collateral assets Immovable property preferred; equitable mortgage standard
Revenue Stability
3 years of audited financials with consistent revenue Government / PSU customers or long-term contracts add strength
Net Worth
Positive and growing — minimum varies by lender & instrument Debt-to-equity ratio below 3:1 typically preferred
End Use
Clearly defined, specific, and lender-approved use of proceeds Diversion of funds is the most common reason for NPA classification
Existing Obligations
All existing loans must be regular and disclosed fully Undisclosed liabilities are a dealbreaker in lender due diligence

Improving Creditworthiness

Factors That Strengthen
Your Credit Profile

Financial

Revenue Consistency & Growth

Three years of audited revenue growth — even modest, consistent growth — signals business stability far more to a lender than a single year of exceptional performance.

Financial

EBITDA Margin Expansion

Improving operating margins demonstrate management efficiency and pricing power — both critical signals in lender credit committees assessing long-term debt serviceability.

Structural

Clean, Disclosed Balance Sheet

All liabilities — including off-balance sheet obligations, promoter guarantees, and contingent items — must be fully disclosed. Hidden obligations discovered during due diligence are deal-breakers.

Collateral

Quality Security Assets

Unencumbered immovable property in prime locations, high-quality plant and machinery, or assigned lease rentals significantly improve both the quantum and rate of debt available.

Promoter

Promoter Credibility & Net Worth

Lenders in India lend as much to the promoter as to the business. A clean CIBIL record, strong personal net worth, and demonstrated integrity in past credit relationships are non-negotiable prerequisites.

Customer

Long-Term Contracts & Quality Clients

Government, PSU, or blue-chip corporate customers on long-term contracts provide lenders with revenue visibility and counterparty comfort — directly improving debt availability and pricing.
